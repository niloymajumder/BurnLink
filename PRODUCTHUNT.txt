BurnLink is a zero-knowledge file sharing tool built for people who take
privacy seriously.

Every file you share is encrypted directly in your browser using AES-256-GCM
before it ever leaves your device. The server stores only an encrypted blob —
we have no ability to read your data, ever.

TWO SHARING MODES:

🔥 View Once
The recipient gets 90 seconds to view the file in-browser. No download.
No save. The file is permanently deleted from our servers the moment they
close it or the timer expires. Screenshots are actively deterred with DOM
clearing, watermarking with their session fingerprint, and screen-change
detection.

📥 One-Time Download
The link burns after the first download. Visit twice and you get a 404.

BUILT-IN SECURITY:
  • Browser-side AES-256-GCM encryption (your key never leaves your device)
  • Password protection with brute-force lockout (3 attempts → 10 min lock)
  • CSP nonces, security headers, rate limiting, UUID validation on every request
  • No accounts, no tracking, no logs
  • Open source — audit it yourself

PERFECT FOR: sharing credentials, legal documents, private photos, API keys,
contracts — anything you'd normally send over email and immediately regret.

Hey PH! 👋 I built BurnLink because I was tired of sharing sensitive files
over email, Slack, or Google Drive — platforms that store everything forever
and can read it.

The core idea: your browser encrypts the file, we store only the ciphertext,
and the link kills itself after use. Even if our database was dumped, there's
nothing readable in it.

The "View Once" mode was the hardest to get right — the challenge isn't just
deleting the file server-side, it's making sure the content can't be trivially
screenshot or saved client-side while still being usable.

It's fully open source: github.com/Joy-Majumder/BurnLink

Would love feedback — especially from security folks. What would you want in v2?
